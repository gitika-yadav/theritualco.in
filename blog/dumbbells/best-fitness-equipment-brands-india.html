Best Fitness Equipment Brands in India (2026)

India's fitness equipment market has grown fast over the past few years, and so has the number of brands competing for attention. Some specialize in heavy-duty commercial gym machines. Others focus on affordable home fitness basics. A smaller, newer group is building equipment specifically for people who want their home gym to look as good as the rest of their living space.

The "best" brand depends entirely on which of those you're shopping for. This guide breaks down the leading names in each category so you can find the right fit, not just the most popular name.

How to Choose a Fitness Equipment Brand

Before comparing brands, it helps to be clear on what you're actually optimizing for:

  • Use case: Commercial gym, home setup, or a specific practice like pilates or yoga
  • Budget: Entry-level, mid-range, or premium
  • Space: How much room you have, and whether equipment needs to stay visible or can be stored away
  • Durability and after-sales support: Especially important for larger, motorized equipment like treadmills
  • Aesthetics: Increasingly relevant as home fitness spaces double as living spaces

Best Brands by Category

Best for Commercial and Heavy-Duty Gym Equipment

Jerai Fitness is one of India's oldest and most established names in the category, manufacturing out of Mumbai since 1994 and supplying equipment to gyms across the country. PowerMax Fitness and Cosco are also widely used by commercial gym owners, known for broad catalogs spanning cardio and strength equipment built for heavy daily use across many users.

If you're setting up a commercial facility or a serious home gym that needs to handle frequent, heavy use, these brands prioritize durability and load capacity above all else.

Best for Affordable, Mass-Market Home Fitness

Decathlon remains one of the most widely used brands for home fitness in India, covering nearly every equipment category — dumbbells, yoga mats, resistance bands, and more — at accessible price points with consistent availability both online and in physical stores. Boldfit has also built a strong presence in this space, particularly for fitness accessories and gear sold through e-commerce.

These brands are a solid starting point if your priority is getting functional equipment quickly and affordably, without much focus on design.

Best for D2C Home Fitness Specialists

A newer wave of India-based D2C brands has emerged specifically around home workout equipment. Cultsport (from the Cult.fit ecosystem) offers a wide range of home fitness gear backed by an established fitness brand. Slovic has grown rapidly in the affordable fitness equipment category, building momentum around dumbbells, resistance bands, and home workout accessories. Flexnest focuses on a mix of smart and non-smart home fitness equipment, including app-connected training content.

These brands sit between the budget mass-market options and premium international equipment, generally offering better price-to-quality value than legacy commercial brands repurposed for home use.

Best for Aesthetic, Design-First Home Strength Training

This is the newest and smallest category, but a fast-growing one — brands building fitness equipment that's meant to be seen, not stored away. The Ritual Co is part of this space, designing silicone capsule dumbbells specifically for women building home strength and pilates practices who want their equipment to fit the rest of their home, not just their workout.

We're a newer name in Indian fitness equipment compared to the established players above, but if soft, pastel-toned design and a smaller, considered product range (rather than a sprawling catalog) is what you're looking for, this is the category worth exploring.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best fitness equipment brand in India?

There isn't a single best brand for everyone — the right choice depends on whether you're building a commercial gym, a budget home setup, or a design-led home fitness space. Each category has different leaders.

Is Decathlon good for home gym equipment?

Yes, Decathlon is widely used in India for affordable, functional home fitness equipment across nearly every category, from dumbbells to yoga mats to resistance bands.

What is the difference between commercial and home fitness equipment brands?

Commercial brands build for heavy daily use across many users, prioritizing durability and load capacity. Home fitness brands typically prioritize affordability, space efficiency, and increasingly, design and aesthetics for equipment that lives in everyday living spaces.

Are Indian fitness equipment brands as good as international ones?

Indian fitness equipment brands have improved significantly in recent years, with several domestic manufacturers and D2C startups now offering quality comparable to international options at more accessible price points.
